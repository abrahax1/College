Pieces

  1. King: The most important piece that must be protected at all costs; if the king is captured, the game is lost.

    Queen: The most powerful piece on the board; it can move in any direction and is crucial in attacking the opponent's position.

    Bishop: A piece that can only move diagonally; it is important in controlling the center of the board and attacking the opponent's pieces.

    Knight: A piece that moves in an L-shape and is unique in its ability to jump over other pieces; it is often used to attack the opponent's pieces from unexpected angles.

    Rook: A powerful piece that can move horizontally or vertically; it is typically used to control open files and attack the opponent's position.

    Pawn: The weakest piece on the board, but can be very powerful when it reaches the opponent's side of the board and is promoted to another piece; it is important in controlling space and creating opportunities for other pieces.

    (Reference)
